# MOF-Derived S-Scheme Heterostructured Photocatalyst for H₂ and CO₂ Conversion

[![GitHub license](https://img.shields.io/github/license/nishaangelinem-coder/MOF-Derived-Heterostructured-photocatalyst)](https://github.com/nishaangelinem-coder/MOF-Derived-Heterostructured-photocatalyst/blob/main/LICENSE)
[![ResearchGate](https://img.shields.io/badge/Research-Photocatalysis-blue)](https://github.com/nishaangelinem-coder/MOF-Derived-Heterostructured-photocatalyst)

This repository contains the complete experimental dataset, characterization figures, and performance analysis for the development of a **MOF-derived S-scheme heterostructure** (α-Fe₂O₃ / NiFe₂O₄) designed for enhanced photocatalytic hydrogen evolution and CO₂ reduction.

## 🌟 Research Highlights

- **Synergistic Heterostructure:** Successfully synthesized an S-scheme heterostructure derived from Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs).
- **Superior H₂ Evolution:** Achieved a hydrogen evolution rate of **~2340 µmol g⁻¹ h⁻¹**.
- **Effective CO₂ Reduction:** Demonstrated CO evolution rate of **~58.7 µmol g⁻¹ h⁻¹**.
- **Exceptional Stability:** Maintained over **95% performance retention** after 5 cycles of continuous photocatalytic operation.
- **Enhanced Charge Separation:** Validated S-scheme charge transfer mechanism through XPS and EIS analysis, minimizing recombination.

## 📊 Key Results

### 1. Structural Characterization
XRD and Scherrer analysis confirm the formation of α-Fe₂O₃ and NiFe₂O₄ phases with reduced crystallite sizes in the heterostructure, suggesting intimate contact and high surface area.

### 2. Optical & Electrochemical Analysis
- **Bandgap Tuning:** UV-Vis DRS analysis reveals significant visible-light absorption.
- **Charge Dynamics:** EIS Nyquist plots show reduced arc radius for the heterostructure, indicating lower charge transfer resistance.

### 3. Stability
Long-term stability tests show minimal structural changes and consistent gas evolution rates, making this material a viable candidate for sustainable energy applications.

## 🛠 Usage & Data Analysis
The raw data files are provided in `.csv` format for easy integration into OriginPro, MATLAB, or Python scripts. The `processed_data/` folder includes fitting parameters used in the manuscript.
